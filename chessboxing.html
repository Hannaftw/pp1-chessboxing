A deeper dive into chessboxing

It's finally time to really get to know the sport of chessboxing.

It's history, rules and some fun facts. The founder Iepe Rubingh, is essential to the history of chessboxing, so we will get hyped up with a quote from him that he uttered after his first chessboxing match.

I had been fighting for 45 minutes and I was ready to drop. I mobilised every nerve-ending in my body, and landed a punch. I could see him struggling to find his corner. All I needed was another hit for the victory! ... then the bell rang, and the the boxing round ended. I wanted to scream, and the next thing I saw was the chess table in the ring, and the final round had begun...

We mere mortals may be able to get a sense of what the sport is like from videos, images and stories, perhaps this quote from the founder and first champion can give us insight into the extraordinary experience of stepping into the ring, as well sitting down at the chess board.

The history of chessboxing

Invented in 2003 by the Dutch artist Iepe Rubingh, but how did he come up with the idea? Rubingh was inspired by the 1992 graphic novel "Froid Équateur" by Enki Bilal. In the graphic novel, set in a dystopian future, a version of chessboxing is the most popular sport in the world. After readting this, Rubingh, being an artist decided to create a live version of the sport as a piece of performance art. Luckily, he saw the potential of the sport and decided to create a real life version but without the dystopia. Had it not been for this stroke of brilliance, the history of chessboxing would have ended as a little known piece of performance art .

The rules of chessboxing

General rules

  • The match consists of 12 rounds, 6 rounds of chess and 5 rounds of boxing. It starts with a round of chess and then alternates between chess and boxing.
  • There is a break of 1 minute between the rounds, to allow the players to change their gear. It would maybe be a bit impractical to play chess wearing boxing gloves, hence the players are allowed to remove them during the chess rounds.
  • A winner can be declared and the match end in 4 different ways:
    • Checkmate - during a chess round
    • Knockout - during a boxing round
    • Technical knockout - during a boxing round
    • The opponent exceeds the time limit in chess - this is for the whole match, not just one round.
  • If all rounds are completed and none of the players have won through any of these ways, a sixth and last round of boxing takes place and the player with the most boxing points from the whole game wins. If it's still a tie, the player with the black pieces wins.
  • There are separate referees for the rounds of chess and boxing, having these separate referees is essential. Judging any of the two events of the match require a specific set of expertise and focus. By dividing the responsibilities it helps to ensure the integrity and fairness of the separate events and the whole match.

Chess Rules

  • Each player has a total of 12 minutes to make all their moves. If all 6 rounds are played, that means each player has 2 minutes per round.
  • There is a separate referee in the chess rounds who keeps track of the time, makes sure chess rules are followed and .
  • Speed chess is the variant of chess that is played in chessboxing. The integration of speed chess is crucial because it ensures the competition moves at a brisk pace, maintaining the dynamic and intense pace of the match. This creates a balance between physically demanding boxing and strategic and time sensitive chess. Making chessboxing a truly unique and demanding sport.

Boxing Rules

  • The boxing rounds have a separate referee, their responsibilties include:
    • make sure that the rules of boxing are followed
    • ensuring the safety of the players
    • counting down when a boxer is down to determine a knockout
    • interveneing should the there be a violation of the rules
    • keeping track of the time during the boxing rounds
  • The boxing rounds are 3 minutes each.

Trivia and fun facts

  • The popular Netflix miniseries “The Queens Gambit” is dedicated to the founder Rubingh. He is credited as an “On-Set Chess Consultant” for the series.
  • In the 1992 graphic novel "Froid Équateur" by Enki Bilal the rules were slightly different. A whole boxing match was played before the chess game. Rubingh thought that this was impractical and adjusted the rules according to his vision.
  • The first chessboxing win was due to one of the players exceeding the time limit in chess.
  • The chess is played on board specially designed for chessboxing, exactly how it differs from a regular board is a little unclear. It has the same dimensions as a regular board, eight by eight squares, and the players have the same set up of pieces as in regular chess. The chess clock however is not the same as a regular chess clock, it is adapted for speed chess.
