An introduction to chessboxing

Ok, what is it, really? The short answer is: A hybrid sport that combines alternating rounds of boxing and chess in the most glorious fashion. But as you will learn, under the surface, it is so much more than that.

Who would think of such a thing? A genious or a madman? Perhaps someone who created a bridge between madness and genious? Not being defined as either but as genious, one with some crazy tendencies. The sport is a result of more than one creative mind. It all began when a Dutch performance artist got his hands on a French graphic novel set in a dystopian future. In this future, a sport similar to our modern version of chessboxing was the most popular sport. The artist was so intrigued by this sport that he decided to make it a reality, first as a piece of performance art, since he was an artist after all. But it did not stop there, he was so dedicated that he created a foundation for the sport and made it into what it is today.

Are there people that are actually partaking in this sport? Yes, there are plenty and the number keeps growing. There are several amateur clubs but also events where the participants are on professional level. These official international competitions managed by The World Chessboxing Organization (WCBO). To compete professionally a player needs to have both knowledge and experience of chess and be deemed physically fit enough for boxing.

How does one triumpf? The short answer that we will stick to at the moment before going more in depth on the page dedicated purely to chessboxing, is: by knocking out your opponent in the boxing ring or defeating them on the chessboard.
